How to quantify Criticality, Occurrence and Recovery

Recovery

Consider the likelihood of control systems detecting the failure in the SmartGrid component and how long it takes for the cause to be identified and recovery procedures to commence. Accordingly, assign the failure a recovery/detection number from 1 to 10:

  • 1. Almost certain detection
  • 2. Very high chance of detection
  • 3. High chance of detection
  • 4. Moderately high chance of detection
  • 5. Moderate chance of detection
  • 6. Low chance of detection
  • 7. Very low chance of detection
  • 8. Remote chance of detection
  • 9. Very remote chance of detection
  • 10. Uncertain detection or no control systems

Failure Modes, Effects and Criticality Analysis

This page will allow you to ascertain the software risks associated with a SmartGrid deployment through the use of the FMECA methodology. The tool below is designed to help you reason about where to invest in securing your SmartGrid infrastructure. It performs risk calculations based on your input and reveals where your grid's strong and weak points are in the facea of a software failure or cyber attack.

Review the list of threats and choose a failure mode for each one. Then, input criticality, occurence and recovery ratings for each one. The chart on the right side will be updated in real time, showing you the Risk Priority Numbers associated with each threat. The higher the number, the larger the impact that potential failure might have on your business.
